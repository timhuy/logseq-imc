		- ### **1. Understanding Current Tax Behavior**
			- In the existing Canadian invoices:
				- Taxes are shown **only at invoice total level** (GST, HST, QST, PST).
				- Percentages can be inferred by auditors since CRA checks invoice‑level totals, not line‑level details.
		- ### **2. Requirement Debate: How Much Tax Detail Should Be Displayed?**
			- Participants discussed the balance between:
				- **Statutory minimum requirement**
					- Invoice total tax is legally sufficient.
				- **Customer expectations & operational clarity**
					- Showing more detail helps partners reverse‑engineer tax calculations.
					- Avoids extra work for controllers and customers when verifying discrepancies.
		- ### **3. Options Evaluated for Line‑Level Tax Display**
			- Key considerations:
				- #### **a. Showing aggregated line‑level tax**
					- Simple approach: show total tax per line.
					- Question: is this enough to help customers understand discrepancies?
						- Missing context: **jurisdiction** (province) and **tax type**.
				- #### **b. Showing tax type breakdown per line**
					- Example: for one line, show GST + HST, or QST + PST.
						- Maximum identified for Canada appears to be **two applied taxes per line**.
					- Proposal:
						- One column for tax type(s),
						- One column for tax percentage(s),
						- One column for tax amount(s).
				- #### **c. Adding more columns is discouraged**
					- The PDF layout is already extremely dense for Canada.
					- Increasing columns would break formatting.
					- Alternative proposed:
						- Display **multiple tax entries within the same line block**, rather than adding new columns.
						- Example:
							- Line 1: GST
							- Line 1: HST
							- (Still treated as one product line visually.)
		- ### **4. Missing Element: Tax Jurisdiction**
			- If customers are expected to validate calculations, they must know **which province’s rules were applied**.
			- Aggregated tax totals without jurisdiction info could lead to confusion.
			- Raised questions:
				- Should the invoice explicitly show province/state?
				- Should it be inferred from customer address or explicitly listed in a field?
		- ### **5. Comparison with Other Regions**
			- EU VAT invoices typically show breakdowns clearly (e.g., 6%, 12%, 21%).
			- Canada & U.S. are more complex with multi‑level taxing authorities.
			- Must avoid clutter while still providing clarity.
			- Competitor invoices and real customer examples are needed for benchmarking.
		- ### **6. Consolidated vs. End‑Customer Invoice Templates**
			- For consolidated reseller invoices:
				- Multiple contracts may share the same tax rates.
				- Breakdown remains similar, just with more line items.
			- For end‑customer invoices:
				- Same layout but fewer lines expected.
				- Each line will have the same tax combination for that end customer.
		- ### **7. Bottom‑of‑Invoice Totals**
			- Current Canadian layout shows:
				- GST + HST combined totals
				- QST + PST totals
			- Requirement:
				- Add additional lines when new combinations exist.
				- Must always match the sum of all line‑level amounts.

				  ---
		- ### **2. PanEuro Operating Model (Currency & Intercompany)**
			- A deep explanation was provided of how **PanEuro**, a German‑registered entity with a massive central warehouse (Straubing), manages stock and currency:
			- #### **2.1 Vendor Currency Logic**
				- PanEuro only buys stock in **two currencies**:
					- **USD**
					- **EUR**
				- It always sells to Ingram country entities in **the same currency it purchased in**.
					- Buy USD → Sell USD
					- Buy EUR → Sell EUR
				- This ensures:
					- No FX differences for PanEuro on stock transactions
					- Countries handle FX locally when customer billing is in a different currency
			- #### **2.2 Country-Level FX Mechanism**
				- Countries receiving stock apply their own FX conversion:
					- Example:
						- France buys from PanEuro in **USD**
						- France sells to customers in **EUR**
					- FX conversion happens **locally**, using the standard FX model (current SAP/IMPULSE behavior).
			- #### **2.3 Inventory Transfer Logic**
				- Inventory transferred from PanEuro to a country:
					- **Never appears as inventory** on the country’s books
					- It only exists for seconds as stock is transferred & immediately sold
					- Avoids country-level inventory reporting
					  
					  ---
		- ### **3. FX Rate Handling, Thresholds & Hedging**
			- #### **3.1 FX Rate Locking**
				- Architecture proposed using:
					- **Spot rate** as baseline
					- **Ingram-held rate** with a tolerance threshold (e.g., ±2%)
					- Rate is locked for the customer **from order to invoice**, even if delivery is weeks/months later
				- This aligns with hedging strategies and forward‑looking FX controls.
			- #### **3.2 Differences from Current System (IMPULSE / Legacy)**
				- Current EMEA behavior may **not** lock rates correctly for:
					- Cost of goods sold (COGS)
					- Some legacy FX treatment
				- France and other countries sometimes see mismatches between:
					- Order date rate
					- Invoice date rate
				- Tim notes this may be **fixed in SAP 3.0**, but needs verification.
				  
				  ---
		- ### **4. Impact on Stock, Backorders & Timing**
			- Back-to-back orders create timing gaps:
				- Order placed today
				- Delivery/transfer happens days later
			- FX challenges arise due to rate fluctuations between:
				- Order date
				- Country transfer date
				- Customer invoice date
			- PanEuro always avoids FX impact itself; the country absorbs it.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **5. Special Regional Models**
			- Several exceptions exist and must be documented:
			- #### **5.1 Sunrise Model (Germany / Switzerland / Austria)**
				- PanEuro invoices **Ingram Germany** → Germany invoices Switzerland & Austria
				- Currency behavior depends on:
					- Vendor currency
					- Local billing requirements
				- Requires confirmation with:
					- **Michael Hornberger**
			- #### **5.2 Nordic Model (Denmark / Finland / Sweden / Norway)**
				- Additional rules or multi-leg flows may exist
				- Requires validation with:
					- **Josep Lloansi** (Nordics CFO)
			- #### **5.3 Poland**
				- Not part of Sunrise or Nordics
				- Uses hybrid model:
					- Mainly purchases via PanEuro
					- But still has some **local vendor contracts**
				- Uses different local ERP (Exacta), so more complexity.
