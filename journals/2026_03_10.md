	- ## **Discussed**
		- ### **1. Purpose of the Session**
			- This was a **persona‑level interview session** to understand:
				- Who uploads rate configuration?
				- What information they provide (thresholds, buffers, country lists, volatility considerations)?
				- What workflow and UI/UX Treasury expects?
			- Critical because the project is **high‑priority** and requires a committed delivery date.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **2. Ownership of Held‑Rate Configuration**
			- Upload/ownership sits mainly with **Treasury**, supported by Regional & Local CFE teams.
			- Workflow described:
				- Treasury creates the rate file.
				- Regional + Local CFE validate and approve thresholds/buffers.
				- Treasury sends final file.
				- System consumes it; no further approvals required.
			- **Sales / Go‑to‑Market teams must NOT edit any rates**.
				- They may only be informed by the CFE teams as needed.
				  
				  ---
		- ### **3. Expected User Experience (MVP Scope)**
			- Treasury expects the following capabilities when uploading configurations:
			- **View current thresholds** already in the system.
			- **Edit or re‑upload** updated configurations.
			- **View historical changes** (e.g., last 6 months).
			- **See activation timing** (when changes will take effect).
			- Possibly: Approval workflow (not required for MVP because approvals happen before file submission).
			  
			  An **Excel upload** is fully acceptable for MVP.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **4. Excel Format for Configuration**
			- Brad will deliver the template. Expected structure:
				- Currency
				- Threshold
				- Buffer
				- Line of business (current assumption: subscriptions first, later TS and other LOBs)
				- Notes/comment field for changes
			- Future state:
				- Ability to map product groups beyond LOB1/LOB2 when non‑cloud or TS products join (e.g., Palo Alto, hardware subscriptions).
				  
				  ---
		- ### **5. Where Will This Be Hosted?**
			- Teams discussed hosting in:
				- **Xvantage Page** (role‑based, controlled access)
				- Or **GPE** (price‑ops tooling)
			- Given user persona (Treasury) and local finance
				- **Xvantage Page self‑service** appears most aligned, but final decision pending detailed design sessions with UX and architecture.
				  
				  ---
		- ### **6. Path for Consuming Rates**
			- Treasury uploads → platform → GPE consumes thresholds/buffers → held rate is computed accordingly.
			- Held rate generation must match consumption rules for subscriptions in the MVP and later TS.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **7. Timing of Rate Activation**
			- Key clarifications:
				- Changes should **activate at midnight local time** on the upload day.
				- Treasury prefers immediate activation ONLY if a threshold breach happens — but for MVP, **activation at midnight** is acceptable.
				- Bloomberg feed considerations:
					- Bloomberg rates arrive at **8:30 AM** but are loaded in systems at **11 PM** to avoid intraday fluctuations.
					- Held rate activation should **not conflict with Bloomberg loading**, but both processes can run independently if the system stores activation timestamps.
					  
					  ---
		- ### **8. Threshold Behavior & Notifications**
			- Held rates only change when thresholds are breached.
			- Sales teams should be aware of impacts but **not directly notified by the system**.
			- Local CFE teams will communicate impacts to Go‑to‑Market.
			- In future, teams want visibility on how close the currency is to breaching thresholds for proactive customer management.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **9. Frequency & Process**
			- Treasury expects a **monthly cadence**:
				- File delivered a few days before month end.
				- Updated rates uploaded before the first business day.
			- Activation still controlled by system logic (midnight activation).
			  
			  ---
		- ### **10. Future UX / Research**
			- After MVP:
				- UX team (Alexi & Belana) will conduct interviews to design enhanced views (dashboards, breach indicators, history timelines).
				- Explore surfacing applied rates to internal users (but **not sales editing rights**).
				  
				  ---
	- ## **Next Steps**

		- Treasury + Regional & Local CFE will review and finalize monthly cadence process.
		- Product/Engineering to:

		  ---
	- ## **Discussed**
		- ### **1. Overview of Demo Scope**
			- Purpose: Provide a demonstration to Product & ISR so **IST can begin**.
			- Focus areas:
				- Renewal quantity modification
				- Discount code behavior
				- Renewal preview data sourced from Adobe
				- UI experience and loading feedback
				- Validation of order creation & subscription updates
				  
				  ---
		- ### **2. Renewal Quantity & Discount Logic**
			- Renewal data (quantities, flex discounts) is fetched **directly from Adobe**.
			- Current issues:
				- Flex discount not always received or displayed correctly.
				- UI and backend misalignment on discount selection.
				- Fixes are already “in pipeline”.
			- Auto‑selection of discounts must **not** occur during renewal modification;
			  
			  Auto‑selection occurs **only** at the actual renewal event (3 days prior), if no user input exists.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **3. UI Discussion & UX Improvements Needed**
			- No spinner/loading indicator shown during data retrieval;
				- Team agrees this must be added for customer experience.
			- Disclaimer about pricing appears at the bottom of the screen only—concerns raised:
				- Might be missed by users, especially with long lists.
				- Should be moved higher or refined.
				- Recommendation from Tim: disclaimer must clearly state pricing is **provided by Adobe**, not Ingram.
				  
				  ---
		- ### **4. Pricing Behavior (Critical Point)**
			- Renewal pricing shown is **always based on current Adobe price lists**.
			- Adobe does not support returning future pricing in API preview calls.
			- Therefore, XVS cannot show “contracted renewal price”—only what Adobe returns.
			- A disclaimer must explain this limitation.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **5. Add‑More‑Products Logic**
			- Adding products is only allowed in the **last 30 days** before renewal.
			- Button is greyed out outside that window; UX hint appears when hovering.
			- Test examples used during demo were outside window, causing confusion.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **6. Renewal Order Creation & Flags**
			- Some subscriptions in the environment **did not generate renewal or billing orders** due to missing configuration flags.
			- Migration plan required:
				- Only certain Adobe subscriptions will be moved to the new renewal logic initially.
				- Rollout will be phased and controlled.
			- IST/UAT teams need:
				- Documentation listing **which SKUs/subscriptions** are correctly configured for testing.
				  
				  ---
		- ### **7. Price Level Hint Behavior**
			- Price level hints shown in UI refer to **current subscription**, not renewal.
			- Renewal-level pricing for quantity changes must reflect **Adobe’s renewal preview response**.
			- Team will validate this in IST.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **8. Line‑Level Return for Renewal/Billing Orders**
			- New functionality allows **line-level returns** for renewal-related orders (previously full return only).
			- Button for “Start Return” not visible during demo → must be implemented/fixed.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **9. Logical Behavior: Quantity Change vs Renewal Quantity**
			- If user **manually sets** renewal quantity → that value is locked and NOT affected by change orders.
			- If user **does not** set renewal quantity → change orders **will adjust** renewal quantity automatically.
			- Team suggested adding UI messaging to avoid confusion.
				- Implementation would require backend support; added to backlog for future consideration.
				  
				  ---
		- ### **10. UAT & Rollout Preparation**
			- Renewal scenarios are **time‑sensitive**.
			- Test data must be created and tested within days to avoid missing renewal windows.
			- Tim emphasized need for:
				- Early planning with countries
				- Clear test cases
				- Navigator ticket updates
				- IST demo recordings and links
			- Coordination between Abhinav, Ad Slager, and country contacts is required.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **11. Outstanding Issues Identified**
			- Missing flex discount propagation.
			- No loading spinner during data fetch.
			- Price level hint not aligned with renewal preview.
			- Renewal/billing orders missing for some test subscriptions due to flags.
			- UI disclaimers need revision and visible placement.
			- “Start Return” not available where expected.
