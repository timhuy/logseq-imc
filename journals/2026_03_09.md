	- ## **Discussed**
		- ### **1. Difficulty Identifying Deal Analyzer Tickets**
			- Deal Analyzer requests are difficult to locate because they are not tagged consistently.
			- A Navigator **platform initiative tag exists for “Deal Analyzer”**
			  
			  → Teams should use this tag to make items discoverable.
		- ### **2. Carly’s Context & Pain Points**
			- Carly’s team manages **post‑sales operations** for vendors such as NetApp and Modine.
			- **Multi‑period billing** (e.g., 3‑year deals billed annually) is the worst pain point.
			- Current workarounds include:
				- Multiplying quantities (e.g., QTY 5 × 3 years = 15).
				- Multiplying lines (3 lines of QTY 5).
			- These lead to errors, heavy manual work, and confusion.
		- ### **3. XVS Current State & Limitations**
			- XVS was expected to eventually solve multi‑period issues, but:
				- Multi‑period support is **not active today**.
				- XVS onboarding is **on hold** (global pause).
				- Vendor onboarding requires **customized SKU structures** (monthly, yearly versions).
				- Vendor‑specific behavior makes onboarding extremely complex.
		- ### **4. Orders Team Input**
			- Hybrid annuities (mix of TS + cloud on same order) may be targeted for **Q2**, but:
				- Unclear which team (Orders, Quotes, Grabber) will take responsibility.
				- No guaranteed timeline.
		- ### **5. Deal Analyzer Limitations**
			- Deal Analyzer enforces deal checks (credit, finance, legal, accounting, IMFS).
			- XVS **only does credit check**, none of the others.
			- Deal Analyzer does NOT handle:
				- Billing logic
				- Vendor invoicing
				- Multi‑year billing calculations
			- Therefore, Deal Analyzer cannot currently solve the billing issues Carly described.
		- ### **6. Impulse & Legacy Problems**
			- Many annuity processes (e.g., Palo Alto) are extremely outdated:
				- Order is keyed in full
				- Then credited
				- Then rebilled annually
				- Child accounts are used for year 2 and 3
			- Impulse was designed in the **1970s** for hardware, not subscriptions → deeply unsuitable.
		- ### **7. Vendor Onboarding to XVS – Why It’s So Hard**
			- XVS requires vendors to align to:
				- Specific SKU formats (monthly vs annual SKUs)
				- Specific transaction flows
				- API or file‑based integration
			- Vendors frequently resist changing their business model.
			- Many vendors require Ingram to conform to *their* data and billing structure.
		- ### **8. Long‑Term Strategy (2‑Year Timeline)**
			- A hybrid XVS + Deal Analyzer solution is being planned:
				- XVS → front‑end ordering
				- Creates a Deal Analyzer ID
				- Deal Analyzer runs the compliance workflow
			- Full integration realistically **~2 years away** (post‑global rollout + LDP integration).
		- ### **9. Skepticism From Teams**
			- Teams noted:
				- Vendors have been told “you’re next to be onboarded” for **6+ years**.
				- Almost no vendors have successfully onboarded into XVS in that time.
			- Confidence in onboarding timelines is very low.
		- ### **10. Leadership Priority & Squads**
			- Multi‑period billing + inconsistent vendor processes are now recognized as a **top priority**.
			- New squad structures and billing remediation programs are being staffed to stabilize:
				- Billing accuracy
				- Revenue reporting
				- Compliance requirements (60‑day clean window)
		- ### **11. Short‑Term Help**
			- Carly will send her pain points via email.
			- Deal Analyzer team will investigate whether **incremental improvements** are possible before long‑term integration.

	- ## **Discussed**
		- ### **1. Tax Compliance Changes Will Be Significant**
			- Current invoicing uses **aggregated tax calculation** at invoice level; upcoming requirements enforce **line‑level tax visibility**, especially for Canada.
			- Migration from A+ tax handling to **Vertex integration** requires broader platform changes, including changes in order capture (OC) and invoice generation.
			- Additional adjustments will be needed to support new tax types and breakdowns in invoice XML (e.g., multiple tax tags).
		- ### **2. Invoice Template Review**
			- An upcoming session will finalize the **invoice template layout** based on decisions made in previous meetings.
			- Canada and US invoice formats will be reviewed first.
		- ### **3. VDA Invoices & EDI Impact**
			- VDA and EDI formats may require updates because tax changes affect:
				- How amounts are calculated
				- How tax breakdowns are represented inside outbound invoice files
			- The team must assess EDI mappings for CMP. Debbie will verify existing EDI structures.
		- ### **4. Flooring / Financing Considerations**
			- Flooring is *not currently relevant* in the immediate phase for cloud and TS:
				- Only 25 Cisco subscriptions remain in the legacy model and none involve flooring.
			- Flooring might return **in future phases**, so architecture must keep this scenario in mind.
		- ### **5. Vertex Integration + A+ to A++ Migration**
			- Canada requires full Vertex adoption.
			- Vertex‑related changes extend beyond invoicing:
				- OCs
				- Invoice XML
				- Address sourcing
			- The solution will require **ballpark estimation** before contacting Vertex for confirmed estimates.
		- ### **6. Address Logic & End‑User Mapping**
			- Discussion ongoing between end‑user engine and XVS about how address IDs will be sourced.
			- Address changes also impact tax determination and therefore must be included in overall design.
		- ### **7. X4C / Invoicing Team Impacts**
			- Even if no functional changes are requested on the platform UI, X4C invoicing needs:
				- Regression validation
				- Possible updates for line‑level tax behavior
			- Team emphasized not to underestimate the impact:
				- New invoice formatting
				- Line‑level tax visibility
				- Vertex logic
			- If not assessed early, issues may emerge during UAT or IRISKEY phases.
		- ### **8. Platform Display vs. Invoice Display**
			- Business decided **no platform UI change** is required (customers currently don’t see detailed tax breakdown there).
			- The invoice will provide the detailed breakdown and remains an improvement over competitors.
		- ### **9. Need for Early Technical Discussion**
