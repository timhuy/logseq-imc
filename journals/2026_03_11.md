	- ## **Discussed**
		- ### **1. API Stability & Risks**
		- Microsoft uses **non‑public APIs** for parts of the transfer‑out process.
			- Because these APIs are not officially documented, Microsoft may **change them without notice**, causing breakage.
			- Team must account for this risk when designing integrations.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **2. Can Azure Reserved Instances (RIs) & Savings Plans Be Transferred?**
		- **Yes**, Microsoft allows transferring RIs and Savings Plans to the target distributor.
		- However, several constraints apply:
			- Only **eligible** RIs/Savings Plans will transfer.
			- If an RI is tied to a specific Azure subscription, **that subscription is NOT canceled automatically** in this Epic.
			- The subscription may remain active but become **“orphaned”** (not receiving usage, but still existing in XVS).
			  
			  ---
		- ### **3. Risk of Orphaned Subscriptions**
		- When RIs transfer out, the linked Azure subscription in XVS:
			- Stays active.
			- Will **not** receive further usage after transfer completion.
		- Concern:
			- If Pay‑As‑You‑Go resources remain linked, the subscription may still generate billing.
		- Team must ensure:
			- Any remaining PAYG resources are manually closed or validated before approving transfer.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **4. Transfer Behavior Explained (Important Clarification)**
		  
		  Microsoft does **not** transfer Azure subscriptions themselves.
		  
		  This process only transfers:
		- Reserved Instances
		- Savings Plans
		  
		  Microsoft requires that the **target distributor already has a valid Azure subscription** for that customer before transfer can occur.
		  
		  Therefore:
		- The customer **does NOT recreate their environment**, but
		- The actual Azure subscription **identifier changes**, and
		- Some resources may require migration between subscriptions if not covered by the RI transfer.
		  
		  This requires:
		- Proper reseller guidance.
		- Internal associate validation before approving transfer.
		  
		  ---
		- ### **5. Constraints from Microsoft**
		- Billing transfer happens only at the start of the **next billing period**, not mid‑cycle.
		- If the reseller or support team attempts transfer incorrectly, risk of:
			- Billing leakage
			- Incorrect resource association
			- Failed transfer states
			  
			  Tim emphasizes:
		- This must be documented clearly for internal teams.
		  
		  ---
		- ### **6. Transfer‑Out Initiation Flow**
		  
		  The transfer‑out flow (current MVP) works as follows:
		- **Another distributor** initiates transfer in Microsoft Partner Center.
		- Microsoft sends an **email notification** to the customer (and reseller).
		- Customer or Ingram associate obtains the **Transfer ID**.
		- Transfer ID + Customer Data must be manually entered in **Seamless Move** within XPS.
		- XVS uses this info to request subscription cancellations/suspensions based on transfer status.
		  
		  Important:
		- Without the Transfer ID, **nothing can be done**.
		- No automated polling of Partner Center yet.
		  
		  Team confirms:
		- Automation + unified dashboard is in the **future roadmap**, not part of current scope.
		  
		  ---
		- ### **7. Transfer ID Usage & Cancellation Rules**
		  
		  Upon receiving a cancellation request:
		  
		  XVS validates via Microsoft API:
		- Whether Transfer ID exists for the customer.
		- Whether the transfer is completed.
		- Whether the subscription belongs to the completed transfer.
		  
		  If validation fails:
		- Cancellation request is **rejected**.
		- Subscription remains **active** in XVS.
		- Example:
			- 4 subscriptions requested for transfer
			- Microsoft transfers only 3
			- The 4th cancellation request **fails** and remains active
			  
			  This prevents data corruption and misalignment with Microsoft’s state.
			  
			  ---
		- ### **8. Limitations & Exceptions**
		- Some subscriptions may still receive billing after transfer if the reseller didn’t fully close PAYG resources.
		- Only **Office and Azure** supported in this phase.
		- Connect‑Off must differentiate:
			- Normal cancellations
			- Transfer‑related cancellations (via transfer ID)
		- Access to transfer‑out features controlled via **APS privilege**.
		  
		  ---
		- ### **9. Future Features (Roadmap Preview)**
		  
		  Not part of current delivery but planned later:
		- Dashboard showing:
			- Transfer‑in
			- Transfer‑out
			- Status of each transfer
		- Automated data retrieval from Microsoft (no manual input of Transfer ID)
		- Unified monitoring for cancellations & failure scenarios
